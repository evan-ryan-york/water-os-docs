### Week 4: Technical Planning & Tool Selection

**Day 19-20: Technical Architecture Design**

**System Components**:

**1. Mobile Application** (primary interface for operators)
- Platform: React Native (cross-platform, you know React)
- Why: One codebase for iOS and Android, large community, good performance

**2. Backend Services**
- Platform: Firebase or Supabase
- Why: Managed services, fast setup, real-time sync, authentication built-in
- Comparison:
  - Firebase: More mature, better mobile SDKs, Google ecosystem
  - Supabase: Open source, PostgreSQL (more powerful queries), cheaper at scale
- Recommendation: Start with Firebase for speed, migrate to Supabase if needed

**3. Route Optimization Engine**
- Approach: Custom algorithm + external API
- Algorithm: Traveling Salesman Problem (TSP) optimization
  - Libraries: Google OR-Tools (free, powerful)
  - Custom heuristics for water delivery constraints
- Fallback: Mapbox Optimization API (if custom algorithm too complex initially)

**4. Maps and Geocoding**
- Platform: Mapbox
- Why: Better international coverage than Google Maps, cheaper, good offline support
- Features needed: Geocoding, directions, traffic data

**5. Mobile Money Integration**
- Kenya: M-Pesa Daraja API
- Philippines: GCash API
- Nigeria: Paystack or Flutterwave (aggregators supporting multiple providers)
- Implementation: Start with one country, add others as you expand

**6. Database Schema Design**

```
Users (Operators)
- user_id, name, phone, email, password_hash
- business_name, vehicle_count, subscription_tier
- payment_status, subscription_expires_at

Customers
- customer_id, operator_id, name, phone, address, coordinates
- created_at, last_delivery_date, total_deliveries

Deliveries
- delivery_id, operator_id, customer_id
- scheduled_date, completed_date, liters_delivered
- payment_amount, payment_method, payment_status

Routes
- route_id, operator_id, date
- customers_array (ordered list), total_distance, estimated_fuel
- actual_distance, actual_fuel, optimization_savings

Payments
- payment_id, operator_id, amount, method
- transaction_reference, status, timestamp
```

**Days 22-28: Optimization Algorithm**

Build:
- Integrate Google OR-Tools or Mapbox Optimization API
- Calculate optimized route based on:
  - Distance minimization
  - Traffic patterns (if available)
  - Vehicle capacity constraints
  - Delivery time windows (if specified)
- Display: Optimized route, distance saved, fuel estimated

Algorithm approach:
```python
# Simplified pseudo-code
def optimize_route(customers, vehicle_capacity, start_location):
    # Create distance matrix (customer to customer distances)
    distances = calculate_all_distances(customers)

    # Use TSP solver (Google OR-Tools)
    solution = tsp_solver(distances, start_location)

    # Calculate metrics
    optimized_distance = calculate_total_distance(solution)
    baseline_distance = calculate_naive_route_distance(customers)
    savings = baseline_distance - optimized_distance

    return {
        'route': solution,
        'distance': optimized_distance,
        'fuel_estimated': distance_to_fuel(optimized_distance),
        'savings': savings
    }
```

Test:
- Does optimization actually reduce distance vs. random order?
- How long does calculation take? (should be <5 seconds for 20 stops)
- Are recommendations reasonable (not sending driver back and forth)?

## Risk Management and Mitigation

### Risk Category 1: Product/Market Fit

**Risk**: Operators don't adopt AquaFleet, product doesn't solve real problem

**Likelihood**: Low (after extensive interviews)
**Impact**: Critical (kills entire business)

**Mitigation**:
- ✅ 30+ operator interviews before building
- ✅ Free pilot with 10 operators validates problem/solution
- ✅ Simple MVP (can pivot quickly if needed)
- ✅ Fast iteration cycles (weekly updates based on feedback)

**Pivot Triggers**:
- <5 pilots willing to pay after 90 days
- Churn >15% monthly after 6 months
- NPS <20 consistently

**Pivot Options**:
- Different customer segment (utilities instead of operators)
- Different geography (try Manila if Nairobi doesn't work)
- Different product (quality testing instead of logistics)

### Risk Category 2: Technical

**Risk**: Route optimization doesn't work well, mobile money integration breaks, AI image recognition inaccurate

**Likelihood**: Medium (technical complexity)
**Impact**: Medium (affects quality, but fixable)

**Mitigation**:
- ✅ Use proven libraries (Google OR-Tools) not custom algorithms
- ✅ Test extensively before launch (100+ test cases)
- ✅ Fallback options (manual routing if auto fails)
- ✅ Redundancy (multiple mobile money providers)
- ✅ Monitoring (alerts if systems go down)

**Response Plan**:
- 24-hour bug response for critical issues
- Weekly updates for non-critical issues
- Transparent communication with customers when issues occur
- Backup manual processes while fixing

### Risk Category 3: Financial

**Risk**: Burn through savings before reaching profitability, can't raise capital when needed

**Likelihood**: Low (conservative financial planning)
**Impact**: Critical (run out of money)

**Mitigation**:
- ✅ Bootstrap friendly (zero capital requirement initially)
- ✅ Fast path to revenue (60 days, not 12 months)
- ✅ Maintain 6-12 months runway always
- ✅ Profitability by Month 6-9 (don't depend on fundraising)

**Warning Signs**:
- Burn rate >$3,000/month before revenue
- Revenue growth <20% month-over-month for 3 consecutive months
- Runway drops below 6 months

**Action Plan**:
- Cut non-essential spending immediately
- Accelerate sales (more aggressive outreach)
- Delay hiring until revenue justifies
- Consider small angel round ($50K) if needed in Month 12-18

### Risk Category 4: Operational

**Risk**: Can't handle customer support volume, quality issues with test strips, utility relationships fail

**Likelihood**: Medium (complexity increases with scale)
**Impact**: Medium (affects reputation, fixable)

**Mitigation**:
- ✅ Hire operations manager at $10K MRR (Month 15)
- ✅ Self-service help center (FAQs, videos reduce support tickets)
- ✅ Multiple test strip suppliers (don't depend on one)
- ✅ Over-communicate with utilities (build strong relationships)

**Response Plan**:
- Support SLA: Respond <24 hours, resolve <72 hours
- Quality issues: Immediate recall and replacement
- Utility issues: In-person visits to resolve, don't let email linger

### Risk Category 5: Competitive

**Risk**: Larger company copies your model, traditional utility improves and crowds you out

**Likelihood**: Low initially, Medium later
**Impact**: Medium (market share pressure)

**Mitigation**:
- ✅ Move fast (first-mover advantage, network effects)
- ✅ Build relationships (operators trust you, hard to switch)
- ✅ Data moat (3 years of data hard to replicate)
- ✅ Operational excellence (if you're best, customers stay)

**If Competition Arrives**:
- Differentiate on quality and service (don't compete on price)
- Deepen integration (make switching painful)
- Expand features (stay ahead)
- Consider acquisition if competitor is strong (liquidity event)

### Risk Category 6: Regulatory/Political

**Risk**: Government bans private water operators, mobile money regulations change, data privacy laws

**Likelihood**: Low-Medium (varies by country)
**Impact**: High (could shut down operations)

**Mitigation**:
- ✅ Geographic diversification (4-6 countries by Month 36)
- ✅ Comply with all regulations (don't take shortcuts)
- ✅ Build political capital (serve voters, utilities love you)
- ✅ Transparency (harder to shut down company helping people)

**Monitoring**:
- Track regulatory news in each country
- Join industry associations (early warning)
- Maintain good relationships with officials

**If Regulation Threatens**:
- Pivot to adjacent model (B2B to utilities if direct service banned)
- Exit market if necessary (focus on others)
- Advocate for sensible regulation (use your data/proof)

**This is marathon, not sprint**:
