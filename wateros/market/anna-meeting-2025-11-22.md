## Key Discussion Points

### Part 1: Public vs. Private Depot Classification

**Depot Classification Findings:**
- Some depots operate with "permission" from Ghana Water but aren't strictly public or private
- Truck drivers sometimes fetch water from private houses during shortagesâ€”homeowners who still have water sell to truckers

**Key Distinction Identified:**
- **Public Depots:** Staff are paid by Ghana Water. Revenue goes to Ghana Water. Truckers go to a separate office to check meters and pay directly to GWCL.
- **Private Depots:** Individual owns the license to operate. Pays water bill to Ghana Water per meter reading. Keeps revenue directly and pays own staff.

**Example - Awoshie Depot:**
- Private depot owned by an individual
- Licensed to operate
- Pays GWCL for water like a normal customer (per meter reading)
- Retains revenue and pays staff directly

### Part 3: WhatsApp Broadcast Strategy

**Original Test Concept:**
Get drivers to sign up for a WhatsApp broadcast sharing which depots have water each day.

**The Ask to Depots:**
Each morning, depots send a message on WhatsApp sharing whether or not they have water. WaterOS aggregates and broadcasts to truckers.

**Benefits:**
- Depots get more business/customers
- Truckers know where to find water

**Proposed Experiment - Digital Metering:**
- Pay to install a digital meter on a private depot's pipe
- Provide GWCL with minute-by-minute data from that meter

**Value Proposition to GWCL:**
