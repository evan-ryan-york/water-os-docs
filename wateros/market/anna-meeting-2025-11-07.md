## Key Discussion Points

### Traffic and Construction Challenges

Anna reported severe traffic issues on the Tema-Accra motorway due to ongoing construction:
- The Tema motorway is being expanded from 2 lanes to 3 lanes each direction
- Travel time from Tema to Accra requires leaving at 4-5am to arrive by 8am
- Heavy industrial traffic from Tema's industrial areas compounds the problem
- Anna is using alternative routes (beach road, Sakumono road) to avoid worst congestion
- Many people now using motorbikes due to traffic

### Key Findings from Field Research

**Depot Owner Interview Insights:**
- Depot owners charge less than truck drivers
- Truck drivers charge more based on hours spent on the road and distance
- Pricing is highly variable and unregulated

**Water Crisis in Sakumono/Tema:**
- **Sakumono Community 13 has had NO tap water for ONE YEAR**
- Residents are entirely dependent on private tanker water
- One community water seller (polytank owner) has become the main supplier for the entire community
- Demand is overwhelming - he receives calls every day, tanker drivers often don't answer his calls

**Supply Chain Breakdown:**
- Ghana Water Company knows they can't supply the whole country
- GWCL previously partnered with depot/hydrant owners to extend coverage
- In July 2025, GWCL instructed depot owners to suspend all hydrant operations
- Fleet owners now travel to Weija, Amasaman, or Kwabenya to find water
- Some drivers are getting water from informal borehole operators
- One driver was reluctant to share his water source, thinking Anna was from Ghana Water investigating him

### Critical Insights

**The Core Problem:**
> "The demand is horrible. The issue is not with the demand. But it's with how they can get the water to supply to the people."

**Supply vs Demand:**
- Original hypothesis: Hard to get drivers on platform
- Reality: **Drivers desperately need water sources** - demand far exceeds supply
- Getting drivers on platform will be easy if we can provide reliable water access
- The bottleneck is depot access, not driver willingness

**Water Quality Concerns:**
- Drivers getting water from boreholes of unknown treatment status
- No verification system for water quality
- "You don't even know whether it's treated or not. Just because people need water."

## Ghana Water Company Context

### Non-Revenue Water Crisis

**Critical Statistics:**
- Well-established water utilities: ~5% non-revenue water
- African utilities average: ~20% non-revenue water
- **Ghana Water Company: 50% non-revenue water**

**What This Means:**
> "One out of every two liters that taxpayers pay to treat at the water treatment plant disappears before generating revenue."

**Causes:**
1. Old, leaking pipes
2. Informal, unprofessional connections in informal settlements
3. Illegal tapping
4. Poor infrastructure maintenance

**Investment Barrier:**
- Foreign investors won't invest in Ghana Water until non-revenue water is addressed
- Technology could help (drone surveillance, sensors) but requires capital
- This is THE major barrier to improving municipal water supply

### Long-term Vision

**After WaterOS Success:**
1. Prove we can organize last-mile delivery
2. Build cash reserves and purchasing power
3. Partner with GWCL on non-revenue water solutions
4. Use technology (drones, sensors) to identify leaks
5. Attract foreign investment once we demonstrate capability
6. Expand to help GWCL improve infrastructure

> "If we have half a million dollars that we're able to use to start investing in getting the water infrastructure fixed, Ghana Water will be very interested."
