**Core Strategy:** "Pure SaaS" Pivot (Avoid FinTech Status)

### 1. The Strategic Pivot

To retain 100% equity (or 90% with a partner) and avoid the Bank of Ghana's 30% local equity mandate, you are changing your business classification.

**Old Model (FinTech):** You process payments, hold funds in a wallet, and pay drivers.
- **Risk:** Requires "Enhanced PSP License" + 30% Ghanaian Equity + GHS 2M Integrity Capital.

**New Model (Tech Service Provider):** You provide the software. Paystack processes the payments.
- **Mechanism:** Use Paystack's Split Payment API.
- **Flow:** Customer pays ‚Üí Paystack ‚Üí [Driver 90% / You 10%].
- **Benefit:** You never hold funds. You are exempt from Bank of Ghana PSP licensing.

### 2. Corporate Structure Options

You have two viable paths to satisfy the Ghana Investment Promotion Centre (GIPC).

**Option A: The "Clean" Joint Venture (Recommended)**
- **Structure:** 90% You (US) / 10% Local Partner (Ghanaian).
- **Capital Requirement:** $200,000 USD (Cash or Capital Goods).
- **Pros:** Lower capital barrier; fully compliant; "investor ready" structure.
- **Cons:** You must trust the 10% partner (though they have no veto power).

**Option B: The "Wholly Foreign" Route**
- **Structure:** 100% You (US Entity or Individual).
- **Capital Requirement:** $500,000 USD (Cash or Capital Goods).
- **Pros:** Absolute control; no partner needed.
- **Cons:** Extremely high upfront capital proof required.

**‚ö†Ô∏è The "Trust" Workaround (The Grey Area):**
- **Strategy:** A Ghanaian holds 100% shares but signs a "Trust Agreement" saying you are the beneficial owner.
- **Risk:** Ghana is cracking down on "fronting." If a dispute arises, this contract may be unenforceable. Use only as a last resort.

### 3. The Profit Repatriation Mechanism

Since you are a US-owned business, you need a legal way to move profits from Ghana back to the US without being double-taxed or blocked by the Central Bank.

**The Tool:** Technology Transfer Agreement (TTA).

**How it works:** Your US entity "licenses" the software to the Ghana entity.
- **The Fee:** You charge the Ghana entity a "Royalty" or "Technical Service Fee" (capped at 0‚Äì6% of net sales).
- **Critical Step:** You MUST register this agreement with the GIPC.
- **Duration:** Min 18 months / Max 10 years.
- **Warning:** An unregistered TTA is unenforceable; banks will refuse to wire the funds to the US.

### 4. Operational Order of Events (The "Timeline")

You cannot do Step 4 without Step 2. Do not skip ahead.

**Phase 1: Legal Formation (Months 1-2)**

1. **[ ] Incorporation:** Register Clearwater Ghana Ltd. with the Registrar General's Department (RGD).

   - **Requirement:** They will demand the GIPC Certificate from Step 3.

5. **[ ] Payments:** Activate Paystack Corporate Account.

## Key Takeaways

### Capital Requirements
- **Foreign-only ownership:** $500,000 USD minimum capital
- **With 10% Ghanaian partner:** $200,000 USD minimum capital
- **Payment Service Provider (PSP):** 30% Ghanaian equity requirement + additional capital requirements
- **Capital can include:** Cash, capital goods, vehicles (valued and counted toward requirement)

### Business Classification
- **Tech Service Provider:** Avoid PSP classification by not holding funds
- **Using Paystack:** Keeps WaterOS as tech provider, Paystack handles payments
- **Partnership with Ghana Water:** Won't change classification as long as not acting as PSP

### Technology Transfer Agreement (TTA)
- **Requirements:**
  - Service not available in Ghana
  - Minimum 18 months duration
  - Maximum 10 years duration
  - Must be governed under Ghanaian law
- **Critical:** TTA registration is mandatory and failure to register can be fatal to enforcing agreements

### Registration Process
1. Find 10% Ghanaian partner (if going that route)
2. Register with GIPC (Ghana Investment Promotion Centre)
3. Open Forex and local bank accounts
4. Wire funds to Ghana with "equity" narration
5. Bank confirms funds to BoG (Bank of Ghana)
6. BoG releases confirmation letter
7. Register with Ghana Revenue Authority (GRA)
8. Register with SSNIT (Social Security) - *Only when hiring direct employees*
9. Consider EPA registration if needed

### Alternative Structure
- **Trust Agreement:** Ghanaian nominee shareholder holds shares in trust for foreign owner
- **Risk:** "Fronting" crackdown in certain industries
- **Legitimacy:** Generally acceptable, won't harm Ghana Water partnership prospects

### Capital Usage
- **Can be used:** For operational expenses after confirmation
- **Integrity Capital:** May need to maintain untouchable capital if classified as PSP (amount TBD)

### Staffing Model
- Independent contractors through third-party service: No immediate SSNIT/income tax registration required
- Only need GRA registration for future tax compliance when profitable

### Timeline
- Target: Q4 2026 operational launch
- Legal setup: Multiple steps over 10-12 months
